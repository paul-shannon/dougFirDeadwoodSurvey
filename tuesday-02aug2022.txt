# Bob Edmonds notes on doug fir diseases
# ----- root and butt
# phellinus sulphurascens: aka laminated root rot, yellow ring rot, Douglas-fir less than 80 years
# phaeolus schweintzii: velvet-top fungus, mostly older Douglas-fir
# armillaria ostoyea: Armillaria root disease, all tree species.  mycelium invades the sapwood and is
#    able to disseminate over great distances under the bark or between trees in the form of black rhizomorphs ("shoestrings").
# heterobasidion (annosus) root disease: hard to diagnose, mainly eastside firs? hemlocks > 120 years.
# ----- stem
#  Porodaedalea pini: older Douglas-fir, heartwood but also sapwood, 'red ring rot' or 'white speck'.
#     causes white pocket trunk rot.  conks have pretty concentric red rings.
